COACHING - BATTING

Forward Defence

  • Played to a good ball that threatens the wickets and needs defending, usually full or good length.

  • Head and front shoulder lean towards the line of the ball.

  • Front foot strides towards the pitch of the ball.

  • Front leg bends to control weight, back leg remains straight.

  • Bring bat down vertically and play ball under your eyes. The face of the bat should be angled towards the ground to keep the ball on the ground.

  • Bat and pad should be close together.

Backfoot Defence

  • Played to a good ball that threatens the wickets and needs defending, usually full or good length.

  • Head and front shoulder lean towards the line of the ball.

  • Front foot strides towards the pitch of the ball.

  • Front leg bends to control weight, back leg remains straight.

  • Bring bat down vertically and play ball under your eyes. The face of the bat should be angled towards the ground to keep the ball on the ground.

  • Bat and pad should be close together.
