COACHING - BATTING

Whether you consider yourself a opening batter, a tailender batter, or the one who fields from fine leg to fine leg, everyone has to bat in cricket at some point or the other. Therefore, it is vital that every cricket player knows the fundamentals to batting. Batting is all about scoring runs. When every you go out to bat, your main focus is looking where to score runs. However, not every ball can be scored off and therfore, you need to know when to defend a ball. You also need to know how to identify balls that can be scored off, and how to score off these balls appropriately.

Important Points for Batting

  • Grip: Ensure your grip is comfortable and allows you to play various shots. Refer to video below.

  • Stance: Feet should be shoulder width apart. Weight should be evenly balanced and head should be stable so that eyes are level with the bowler running in.

  • Watch the ball: Watch the ball closely, from the point of release from bowler and play ball under your eyes.

Frontfoot Defence

The frontfoot defensive shot is vertical bat shot played with sole purpose of defending. It is usually played to a good ball that thratens the wickets and needs defending, usually a full or good length ball.

  • Head and front shoulder lean towards the pitch of the ball.

  • Front foot strides towards the pitch of the ball.

  • Present full face of the bat and play ball under your eyes. The face of the bat should be angled towards the ground to keep the ball on the ground.

  • Bat and pad should be close together.

Backfoot Defence

The backfoot defensive shot is vertical bat shot, played with the sole purpose of defence. It is usually played to a ball short of a good length, which is targetting the stumps.

  • Backfoot should move back and across and stay inside the line of the ball.

  • Front shoulder dips forward.

  • Front foot moves back and hips remain side one.

  • Bring bat down vertically with front elbow high.

  • Use full face of the bat to defend the ball. Angle the bat face slightly towards to ground to keep ball on the ground.

Straight Drive

The straight drive is a vertical bat shot, played with the purpose of attacking and scoring runs. It is uaually played to a full or overpitched ball, pitching on the stumps. This shot is hit straight past the bowler and down the ground.

  • Lean into the ball with head and shoulders forward.

  • Bend front knee and keep back leg straight and extended.

  • Play ball under your eyes with full face of the bat.

  • Hit ball straight past the bowler.

Backfoot Punch

The backfoot punch is a vertical bat shot, played with the purpose of attacking and scoring runs. It is usually played to a short pitched ball which is bouncing between knee and waist height.

  • Back foot moves back and across, with backfoot toes facing point region.

  • Front shoulders dip towards ball, to assist in controlling the ball.

  • Front foot strides back to assist with balance .

  • Hands high to get on top of ball and play ball with a high elbow keeping your head still.

Pull Shot

The pull shot is a horizontal bat shot, played with the purpose of attacking and scoring runs. It is usually played to a short pitched ball, pitching on middle, leg or outside leg stump. This shot is played through the leg side.

  • Push backfoot back and across.

  • Keep head still and forward.

  • Hands high to allow for high-to-low swing and to get on top of ball.

  • Arms fully extended through the shot and weight moves in direction of shot.

Cut Shot

The cut shot is a horizontal bat shot played with the purpose of attacking and scoring runs. It is usually played to a short pitched ball, bowled wide or outside off stump. This shot is played through the off side, usually through the point region.

  • Step back and across to reach the ball, with backfoot toes facing point region.

  • Hands high to allow for high-to-low swing.

  • Hit ball through point, with head still and full extension of arms.

  • Transfer weight to the intended direction of shot.

  • Follow through with bat reaching around to opposite shoulder.
