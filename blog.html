Insight - Dealing with an Injury

The past six months, have been full of ups and downs thanks to sustaining a season ending injury. During my representative tournament, I started to experience a slight pain in my foot but knowing the seriousness and importance of the tournament, I decided to push it out of my mind momentarily. After almost 5 months on the sideline, I can safely say that was a big mistake. However, it didn't stop there. Having being told by the doctors I had only sprained my foot, I took two weeks rest and then continued to play club cricket, only making the pain in my foot worse. After a number of ultrasounds, x-rays and CT scans, they confirmed I had a stress fracture which immediately cut my season short. On the bright side, after 135 days of being injured I have finally been given the all clear. After all this time, it's safe to say I am more than ready to get in the nets again. Dealing with an injury has been really difficult at times. From experience, I can say it is really hard being patient, knowing your missing out and at the same time there's nothing you can do but wait it out. Here are some things I have learnt during this time off:

  1. Be Patient: As hard as it is, being patient is so important. Getting back into the game too early can push you back heaps in terms of recovery. It is key to wait until you are painfree and recovered to play again.

  2. Take the time off: During the season, it can get pretty full on. This means when you have an injury which prevents you from playing, you have a lot more time on your hands. No doubt, I would 100% rather be fit and able to play, but there's no point sitting around and getting down about your injury. Take the time off to catch up and do other things. This may be related to cricket or not. For me personally, I got the chance to do both. I used this extra time to catch up with school work, but also spent some time learning more about cricket by watching old games and reading more about cricket.

  3. Be Positive: Think about the future. This could mean thinking long term or short term. Try focusing on next season and what areas you could improve in. It is also important to consider your rehab and how you will work towards getting fit and back into cricket again!

06/08/2019

Article - Steady the Ship Kane!

The crowd roars. The red lights on the bails flicker on. As the opposition rise in a sudden wave of triumph, we groan in agony watching the fielders celebrating and the batsman walking off. However, fans continue to wear their sailor hats with pride and wave their flags. Banners are held high with the words “Steady the ship” painted in black ink. The music erupts in the background, sending the audience into an ecstatic frenzy. Thousands of people watch, clinging to the edge of their seats. A figure emerges out of the pavilion, as a ripple of relief washes over the New Zealand fans. Unfazed by the roaring crowds and the howling opposition, he makes his way to the middle. We ease ourselves back into our seats because Kane Williamson is here, to indeed steady the ship.

Kane Williamson has represented himself and New Zealand in the best manner. Consequently, he has been nominated for the 2020 New Zealander of the Year Award. Williamson’s classy gameplay, his outstanding leadership and remarkable sportsmanship qualities all contribute to his overall character. The New Zealander of the Year Award will acknowledge Williamson’s hard work and give him the recognition he truly deserves.

He walks to the crease and takes guard. His eyes dart around, scanning the field and the gaps. The rest is done simply by him and his bat. The English willow of his bat caresses the leather of the ball, strategically manoeuvring it down to the boundary rope. Silence fills the opposition as they stare, speechless, and in awe of the class player, they see before them.

Williamson not only possesses the ability to play an astonishingly picturesque innings, but also the ability to completely change a game. This has helped him contribute significantly to many Blackcaps victories. Williamson has also been consistently technically correct in regards to his gameplay. In the recent One Day International World Cup, he was recognised as Player of the Tournament, making 578 runs, with a staggering batting average of 82.57. His orthodox technique and consistency are highly commended by coaches and admired by critics. Williamson has represented the country exceptionally, and in recent times, he has even proved the Blackcaps are a force to be reckoned with. He sets a steady example for aspiring cricketers who are seeking to refine their technique. Williamson has been exceptional with the bat and sets a high standard for his team, truly leading from the front.

Our eyes dart back and forth. The South Africans are buzzing and desperate for a win. Their skipper - Du Plessis, hustles around the field hopelessly attempting to figure out how to keep New Zealand below the required total. Williamson glances around the ground, remaining calm despite the building atmosphere. The pressure accumulates as the bowler runs in. Williamson cleanly picks up the slow ball and demolishes it into the stands for six runs, bringing up his century. He celebrates with a simple raise of the bat, knowing the job is not done yet. A well-timed cut off the bat then leads New Zealand to win.

Williamson possesses the ability to remain unbelievably composed but level-headed, despite the incredibly pressurising circumstances he is often faced with. No one would suspect that behind his steely eyes, the cogs are always turning. Constantly analysing the situation, he comes up with savvy tactics to effectively influence the match. Williamson's approach to captaincy has also been sharp, but silent. While many other captains are known for being flamboyant and showy, Williamson’s captaincy has been more unassuming, but effective. “Underdogs” is a rather modest label, one that has been given to the Blackcaps who are often dangerous when underestimated. “When you look at Kane’s captaincy, I think the team has been shaped in his image - pretty low-key and humble.” Said former Blackcap Daniel Vettori. With the bat in hand, advising the bowlers or setting the field, Williamson has truly raised the bar regarding captaincy. Any aspiring athletes can regard him as a role model because of his composure, level-headedness and humility.

The crowd is divided. Half rejoice in celebration, while the rest stop and stare, shocked to witness the devastating outcome. The West Indian players drop to their knees. They hang their heads in disbelief of how close they had come, only to be robbed of any chance of qualifying. Tears escape the eyes of Carlos Brathwaite and drip down his face, soaking his already drenched shirt. Williamson wanders over to Brathwaite and lays a hand on his head. He consoles him as fans watch the heartwarming moment, in genuine admiration of the New Zealand captain.

Kane Williamson displays outstanding sportsmanship qualities. Comforting Carlos Brathwaite was just one of the many instances in which Williamson was commended for his outstanding sportsmanship. Throughout the tournament, he played fair and within the spirit of cricket. Williamson is also known to lead his team to be humble in victory and gracious in defeat. This was evident in the agonising One Day International 2019 World Cup Final against England. Though many would insist that the cup was awarded to England unjustly, Williamson accepted the situation for what it was and even gave England credit for their performance. In the post-match interview, he said “I have spoken a lot about uncontrollables and there were a couple of today, which were hard to swallow. England are deserved winners, take nothing away from them - it’s one of those things.” Any athletes can learn from the exceptional sportsmanship qualities that Williamson displays.

Since the 2019 One Day International World Cup, Kane Williamson has been in the spotlight. In past years, many other New Zealand athletes have been acknowledged for their hard work through the New Zealander of the Year Award. Kane Williamsons classy game play, outstanding captaincy and remarkable sportsmanship qualities have stood out on the world stage. Consistency is maintained through all aspects of his game, making him a sensational role model. Steady the Ship is an understatement for Kane. It is time for our country to recognise Kane Williamson as New Zealander of the Year.
