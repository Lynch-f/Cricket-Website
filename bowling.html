COACHING - BOWLING

Pace Bowling

Pace Bowling is a method of bowling in which the bowler bowls fast or swings the ball. Pace bowlers may also be known as fast bowlers, seamers or swing bowlers.

  • Wrist behind the ball and present ball with the seam upright.

  • Pull down on the back of the ball to create backspin. This helps stablise the ball while it's in the air.

  • Grip the ball with first two fingers slightly apart on the seam and thumb underneath the ball and on the seam. You can experiment to see what is comfortable for you.

  • Run in straigt line towards target and accelerate gradually, keeping both your arms close to your body.

  • All body movements should be directed towards the target.

  • Head should by steady and keep your eyes on the target.

  • Once ball is bowled, follow through in direction of target.

Off Spin Bowling

Off Spin is a variation of spin bowling, which is bowled by a right-handed bowler. This involves the bowler using their fingers to spin the ball from the off side to the leg side for a right-handed batter, or away from the left-handed batter.

  • Present seam horizontally and grip with first two or three fingers spread as widely as possible around the ball.

  • Rest the ball lightly on the thumb.

  • Accelerate gradually towards the crease, keeping arms close to the body.

  • All body movements should be directed towards the target.

  • Keep head steady with eyes fixed on target at all times.

Leg Spin Bowling

Leg Spin is a variation of spin bowling, which is bowled by a right-handed bowler. This involves the bowler using a wrist spin action, which results in the ball spinning away from a right handed batter (from the leg side to off side) or into a left handed batter.

  • Ball should be placed the area when the first two fingers point upwards and the second two fingers point down. This should leave a gap between the ball and the palm.

  • The thumb should rest lightly on the ball and ball should not be gripped too tight, as this causes friction on the ball.

  • Wrist should be cocked and palm should face ground.

  • Accelerate gradually towards the crease, keeping arms close to the body.

  • All body movements should be directed towards the target.

  • Keep head steady with eyes fixed on target at all times.
